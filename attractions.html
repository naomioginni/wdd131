Must-See Attractions

Lagos is a city that never sleeps. Here are the top places you must visit during your weekend trip.

Nature

Lekki Conservation Centre

Escape the city noise and walk the 401-metre canopy walkway — the longest in Africa. Spot green monkeys, crocodiles, and rare birds on guided nature trails through mangrove forests.

Culture

Nike Art Gallery

A spectacular five-story building in Lekki holding one of Africa's largest collections of Nigerian paintings, textiles, batik, and sculptures. The founder, Chief Nike Davies-Okundaye, often visits in person.

Beach

Tarkwa Bay Beach

An idyllic, sheltered beach accessible only by a 10-minute water taxi from Bar Beach. The calm waves make it perfect for swimming, and the vibe is laid-back compared to busier Lagos beaches.

Food

Victoria Island Food Scene

VI is home to hundreds of restaurants serving everything from smoky roadside Suya to high-end Nigerian fusion. Don't leave without trying Buka-style Pepper Soup and fresh Coconut rice.

Shopping

Balogun Market

One of the largest open-air markets in West Africa. Shop for vibrant Ankara fabrics, handmade jewellery, leather goods, and traditional Yoruba attire at unbeatable prices.

Nightlife

Afrobeats Nightlife

Lagos is the birthplace of Afrobeats. Experience the city after dark at iconic venues in Lekki and VI, where local DJs spin Burna Boy, Wizkid, and Davido until sunrise.
